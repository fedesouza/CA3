Wheel of the year

following the Celtic Wheel of the Year, celebrating eight major holidays or sabbats throughout the year. Each Sabbat section will provide you with information on lore and traditions, rituals, activities, and recipes for each Sabbat. If you wish to skip to a certain section, simply click a link on the Sabbat page to jump to the area you want to read. We hope you will enjoy this information and find it helpful as you explore and celebrate the seasons!

The witch year runs from October 31st through October 30th we will follow that time frame with the Sabbat Listings.

Pagan/Wiccan Holidays

Event Date Description
Samhain October 31 - November 1 Celebrating the end of the harvest season and the beginning of winter. It's a time to honor ancestors, reflect on mortality, and thin the veil between the living and the spirit world.
Yule (Winter Solstice) Around December 20-23 Marking the longest night and the return of the sun. Yule is a time for warmth, introspection, and celebrating the rebirth of the sun. Gift-giving and festive lights are common.
Imbolc February 1-2 Recognizing the first signs of spring. Imbolc is associated with purification, fertility, and the Celtic goddess Brigid. It's a time for initiations, creative inspiration, and planning for the future.
Ostara (Spring Equinox) Around March 20-23 Welcoming the arrival of spring. Ostara celebrates balance and the increasing daylight. Activities include planting seeds, decorating eggs, and focusing on personal growth.
Beltane April 30 - May 1 Marking the peak of spring and the beginning of the warmer season. Beltane is a celebration of fertility, love, and the union of the god and goddess. Maypole dancing and bonfires are common.
Litha (Summer Solstice) Around June 20-23 Celebrating the longest day and the height of summer. Litha is a time of abundance, outdoor festivities, and honoring the sun's energy. It marks the turning point toward shorter days.
Lughnasadh August 1-2 Recognizing the first harvest and the sacrifice of the Celtic god Lugh. Lughnasadh is a time for feasting, games, and giving thanks for the abundance of the season.
Mabon (Autumn Equinox) Around September 20-23 Acknowledging the second harvest and the balance between light and darkness. Mabon is a time for reflection, gratitude, and preparing for the winter months. Activities include sharing harvest foods and giving thanks.
